ü•Ñ Understanding Spoon Theory

What is Spoon Theory?

Spoon Theory is a metaphor created by Christine Miserandino to explain the limited energy available to people living with chronic illness. Each "spoon" represents a unit of energy. You start each day with a limited number of spoons, and every activity costs spoons. When you're out of spoons, you must rest.

ü•Ñ How to Use the Spoon Scale (0-10)

0-2 Spoons - Crisis Mode

Severe fatigue. Only essential activities possible. Be very gentle with yourself.

3-5 Spoons - Low Energy

Limited energy. Prioritize only essential tasks. Rest between activities.

6-7 Spoons - Moderate Energy

Manageable energy. Pace yourself throughout the day. Don't overdo it.

8-10 Spoons - Good Energy

Feeling good! Make the most of today, but still listen to your body.

üî¥ Pain Level: How much physical pain are you experiencing? (0=none, 10=severe)

üò¥ Fatigue Level: How tired or exhausted do you feel? (0=energetic, 10=completely drained)

üí° Tips for Tracking

  • Track daily: Log your journal entry at the same time each day (morning works well)
  • Be honest: Rate how you truly feel, not how you wish you felt
  • Look for patterns: Over time, you'll see which medications or activities affect your spoons
  • Share with doctors: Use the calendar view to show your healthcare provider your symptom patterns
  • Adjust plans: Use your spoon count to decide what you can realistically do each day

You know your body best. Spoon Theory is a tool to help you communicate your energy levels and make informed decisions about your daily activities. There's no "right" number of spoons - every chronic illness warrior's experience is unique.
